Neon Bubble

The project is inspired by a genre of music, Citypop, which had demised in the 1990s but has recently returned to the public eye, particularly in the Western Internet world. Citypop is a Japanese adaptation of Western music that originated in the 1970s and 1980s, as a result of Japan's rapid economic development. At that time, Japanese citizens desire for an international lifestyle to celebrate their economic miracle, such as luxury clothing, food, travel and nightlife. In addition, the development of Citypop was also influenced by the development of sound technology in Japan at the time, with Walkmans, built-in cassette recorders and various electronic instruments being mass-produced. Nowadays, noise produced from these players still can be discovered in the re-sampling Citypop songs. It feels like mechanical faults, like an electronic ghost hidden in the frequencies of the music.

Citypop has a retro-futuristic melancholy, a boom time in Japan with its neon-lit metropolises and abundant consumer freedom. The hope then turned into a bubble in the 1990s due to the economic recession. Whereas the original Citypop music represented a vision of the future for the people of the past, the re-sampling that has become popular in recent years is a nostalgia for the future, a longing for the 'future of the past'. The re-popularization of Citypop is a double imagery of East and West, past and present, dreams and everyday life. The reality is a bubble, and the fantasy of the past for the future becomes the subject of nostalgia in the future. The future in the past always full of hope and I want to visualize the hope and sadness.
