Liminal Labyrinth

Video from this Neon Bubble in Vimeo

“Liminal Labyrinth” is an exploration-based adventure VR game where players are invited to explore the dream, memory, liminal space through walking and interact with various places. Inspired by “The Backrooms”, an urban legend and creepypasta about a maze with no end that has empty office rooms in a moist yellow room and hallways. It originated from a post in reddit, and now developed into a web-series sic-fic [1] and also video game called “The Backrooms Game” . According to the legend, people would fall into backrooms when he were out of reality(noclip) in the wrong place. Many of backrooms’ scenes correlate to the notion of liminal space. A physical liminal space is something tangible that you can recognize as a pathway, passageway or portal. These places are transitory, meaning you’re supposed to pass through them on your way to something else. If you rest in a liminal space too long, transitions can become a destination instead [3].

Inspired by Backroom forum, Yume Nikki (2004), Anemoiapolis (2023), and Just More Doors (2022), I intend to design an explorative experience of liminal spaces in virtual reality, creating an immersive experience for audiences and trying to evoke their different perceptions of abnormal space but quite familiar with.
