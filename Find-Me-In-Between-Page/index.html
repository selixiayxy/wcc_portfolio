Find Me In Between

The concept originates from my feeling of time fragment, “I live in a time that does not belong to the present, in the past, in the future, in a parallel time”. Time is often thought of as linear, with a distinct beginning and end. However, physicists and philosophers believe that time may be multidimensional and non-linear. For example, in relativity, time and space are intertwined, and time appears to slow down near black holes. In quantum mechanics, events occur simultaneously, rather than in a fixed order. If time is not linear, can ‘I’ in different time zone meet up in a certain intersection point? Would my body and mind be dual presence in the past and there could be interactions between our future self and our past self? Or just my conscious and soul wandering in the past and our physical bodies would continue to exist in the future timeline? Or neither our physical bodies nor our souls would exist in the past timeline, but rather a copy would be created in a parallel timeline?

The time travel is still an open question for physicist and philosopher, leaving room for imagery. Inspired from films about time travel, I fictionally assume that my body can produce a copy at every second, and my mind and soul can travel back to the past and interact with my copy at that parallel timeline. To visualize this, I’m trying to use montage editing to emphasize discontinuity and the disconnection. The time-image montage is often associated with the use of techniques such as the long take, the close-up, and the non-linear narrative structure, which serves to break the continuity and create a sense of disjunction
